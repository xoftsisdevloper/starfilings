GST (Goods & Services Tax)

GST, or Goods and Services Tax, is a comprehensive indirect tax levied on the supply of goods and services across India. It replaced a complex structure of multiple indirect taxes, aiming to streamline the taxation system, eliminate cascading taxes, and foster economic growth. Unlike its predecessors, GST operates on a dual model, dividing the tax levies between the central and state governments. It encompasses various taxes such as Central Excise Duty, Service Tax, VAT, and others under one umbrella, ensuring uniformity in taxation across states and reducing compliance burdens for businesses. Implemented in 2017, GST has transformed the Indian taxation landscape, promoting transparency, efficiency, and ease of doing business while benefiting both consumers and businesses alike

  2. Casual Taxable Persons

    3. Individuals or businesses supplying goods or services on a temporary basis in a jurisdiction where they don't have a fixed place of business are considered casual taxable persons and must register for GST.

  3. Non-Resident Taxable Persons

    4. Non-resident individuals or businesses supplying goods or services in India are required to obtain GST registration, irrespective of turnover.

  4. E-commerce Operators

    5. E-commerce platforms facilitating the supply of goods or services are required to register under GST, regardless of turnover.

  5. Input Service Distributors (ISD)

    6. ISDs, which distribute input tax credit (ITC) to other units of the same business, must obtain GST registration.

  6. Tax Deductors

    7. Entities responsible for deducting tax at source (TDS) under GST, such as government agencies or specified persons, are required to register as tax deductors.

  7. Tax Collectors

    8. Individuals or businesses collecting tax at source (TCS) under GST, such as e-commerce operators, are required to register as tax collectors.

  8. Inter-State Suppliers

    9. Businesses making inter-state supplies of goods or services are required to register for GST, irrespective of turnover.

  9. Exporters and Importers

    10. Entities engaged in the export or import of goods or services are required to register for GST, as exports and imports are treated as zero-rated supplies under GST.

  10. Voluntary Registration

    11. Entities or individuals not mandatorily required to register for GST may choose to register voluntarily to avail of input tax credit benefits and streamline compliance.
