Mortgage Loan

Mortgage loans are financial instruments used by individuals and businesses to purchase real estate while spreading the cost over an extended period. These loans are secured by the property being purchased, serving as collateral that reduces risk for lenders and typically allows borrowers to access larger amounts of capital than unsecured loans. Mortgage loans come in various types, including fixed-rate mortgages where interest rates remain constant throughout the loan term, and adjustable-rate mortgages where rates fluctuate based on market conditions.
