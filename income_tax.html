TDS Refunds

Tax Deducted at Source (TDS) refunds occur when the tax deducted exceeds the actual tax liability of a taxpayer. This often happens due to high withholding rates or incorrect estimates of income and deductions. To claim a TDS refund, individuals and businesses must file their income tax returns, declaring all sources of income and detailing the TDS amounts deducted.

ITR-1 Return filing

ITR-1, also known as Sahaj, is an Income Tax Return form used by individual taxpayers in India. It is primarily for residents with income up to ₹50 lakh from salary, one house property, and other sources like interest. This form simplifies the filing process for taxpayers with straightforward income structures.

ITR-2 Return filing

ITR-3 Return filing

The ITR-3 form is used by individuals and Hindu Undivided Families (HUFs) in India who have income from business or professional activities. This form is suitable for those who operate as a proprietor or are engaged in a profession, such as doctors, lawyers, or freelancers. It also covers income from salary, house property, and other sources. Filing ITR-3 requires detailed information about the income and expenses related to the business or profession, ensuring accurate calculation of net taxable income. Deductions under various sections of the Income Tax Act can be claimed to reduce tax liability. The form also requires details of any audit conducted under the Income Tax Act, if applicable.

ITR-5 Return Filing

ITR-5 is a specific income tax return form in India designed for firms, LLPs (Limited Liability Partnerships), Association of Persons (AOPs), Body of Individuals (BOIs), and Artificial Juridical Persons (AJPs) that are not required to file their returns using any other form such as ITR-1, ITR-2, ITR-3, or ITR-4. It's a comprehensive form that requires detailed financial information, including balance sheet, profit and loss account, and other financial details.

TDS-Return filing & form-16

TDS (Tax Deducted at Source) return filing and Form 16 issuance are critical aspects of tax compliance for businesses and employers in India. TDS return filing involves submitting details of TDS deducted and deposited to the Income Tax Department periodically, usually quarterly. This process ensures that taxes deducted from various payments like salaries, interest, rent, etc., are correctly accounted for and remitted to the government.

Income tax notice response & appeals

Responding to income tax notices and navigating appeals processes are critical for individuals and businesses alike. When a taxpayer receives an income tax notice, it typically outlines specific concerns or discrepancies found in their tax returns or financial disclosures. Prompt and accurate responses are essential to address these issues effectively. The response process involves gathering relevant documents, analyzing the notice details, and preparing a comprehensive reply that addresses each point raised by the tax authorities. This may include providing clarifications, submitting additional information, or correcting errors in tax filings.

Tax Audit

A tax audit is a detailed examination of an individual's or company's financial records and tax returns by tax authorities to verify compliance with tax laws. Typically initiated when discrepancies or anomalies are suspected, audits can be random or targeted based on specific criteria like high deductions or unusual transactions. During a tax audit, auditors scrutinize income statements, expense records, deductions claimed, and supporting documentation. They may also interview key personnel to clarify financial transactions.
